Perks of acquiring Cyber Intelligence reporting

  • February 1, 2018
  • Security

Security-Intelligence refers to real time monitoring and analysis of critical data that has the potential to impact an organisation, both in terms of reputation as well as capital. Basically it offers a comprehensive insight to all the possible risks and threats to an organisation

CI reporting can be quite beneficial for businesses in a number of ways; foremost and the most obvious advantage of acquiring threat intelligence is quicker detection of threats. The faster an error or intentional move to shred the security can be spotted, the easier it becomes to take situation in control.

Secondly, it reduces the chances of insider threats. Employees holding sensitive data or having access to such data are found to be the most impacted party in case of data breaches. They are the ones who get away with a fraud without grabbing any specific attention for the masks they carry, so it becomes a crucial step to keep a track of insider activities to prevent any cyber attacks.

Thus it significantly reduces the efforts required to keep pace with advancements in threat-agents. Lowering the burden on security officials increases their productivity, thereby enhancing the overall efficiency of the system.

Internet as source was developed with a motive to promote learning and to make information accessible easily. So proper use of internet and other sources of experience like case studies of the ones already been compromised of security in past, is fruitful effort to ensure enhanced security models.

Now that we are aware of the functionalities of threat intelligence, the official who handles the complete process is called a Cyber Intelligence Analyst and possesses skills like Spying and Internet-Security. An analyst sets up strong networking models to obstruct malicious intrusion into their system; he holds great knowledge of technical research and intelligent threat analysis and creates detailed CI report out of them. Apart from bachelors in either computer science or information technology and cyber certifications, an analyst is expected to have intense experience with operating systems, networks, intrusion detection algorithms, firewalls etc. i.e.it takes a lot of time and reputation.

Above all, the analytical and organisational skills are of high demand, to study pinpoint details and take instinct decisions. The job of a threat intelligence analyst requires years of experience and a personality stern enough to make a call in case of cyber attack.
