How are these two different?

  • January 1, 2017

"Information Technology is the business application of Computer Science"

Elaborating this statement, I would begin with the history and evolution of the field of computing over the years: In the early 1960s computing comprised the following degree programs: On a Windows system:

  • Computer Science
  • Electrical Engineering
  • Information Systems
Computer Science dealt with theoretical aspects of programming and developing softwares out of it. While Electrical Engineering focussed on the hardware components related to computing and similar machinery.

Information Systems had nothing to do with developing new technology or any correlation with hardware and software. Information Systems simply used the technology developed by other streams to solve business problems. In-fact, Information Systems had tie ups with business schools and minimal or no interaction with computer scientists and electrical engineers. Later, during the 1970s, with dire need of a sole department for developing computer oriented hardware Computer Engineering emerged out of Electrical Engineering.

By the 1990s Computer Science had developed a considerable body of research, knowledge and innovation of theory and it's practical application to build softwares. And Software Engineering emerged as sub area under Computer Science to develop and maintain software systems that behave reliably and efficiently.

On a parallel track Information Systems was facing a crucial challenge to cope with rapid development and increased complexity of information generated. They were now lacking the skill to make use of newer technology to support organisational efficiency. These problems lead to the rise of of Information Technology as a separate field!

Post the development of Information Technology, Information Systems took a turn and focussed on simply generating information and making best use of it. The strategies to manage and store the information was left for Information Technology. It was IT's responsibility to make sure if an organisation's infrastructure is appropriate and reliable and lead corporations to have a IT department within to ensure the computing infrastructure solved the purpose as and when required by the organisation.

Gradually, Computer Science and Computer Engineering merged as Computer Science and Engineering. And CSE is concerned with design and construction of computers and computer based systems involving study of hardware, software, communication and interaction among them.

No doubt IT and CSE have the same prescribed courses upto an extent of 95% but the approach is different.

IT's job is to understand customer's/corporation's needs and develop programmes that meet them.
